<h2>What is a natural pool?</h2>
<p>A natural pool, also called a biological or ecological pool, uses natural cleaning processes instead of chlorine. Plants, microorganisms, water movement and filtration zones work together to keep the water clear.</p>
<h2>How it differs from a traditional pool</h2>
<p>A traditional pool relies on chemicals to keep the water sterile. A natural pool is built around balance. The water is alive, but controlled through design, circulation and biological filtration.</p>
<h2>Why choose a natural pool?</h2>
<ul><li>No chlorine smell or harsh chemical handling.</li><li>A softer swimming experience for skin and eyes.</li><li>A design that can blend into the garden.</li><li>Lower dependence on ongoing chemical treatment.</li></ul>
<h2>Cost and maintenance</h2>
<p>The initial investment can be higher than a standard pool, especially when the system is custom-designed. Over time, many owners value the lower chemical use, the natural appearance and the way the pool becomes part of the landscape.</p>
